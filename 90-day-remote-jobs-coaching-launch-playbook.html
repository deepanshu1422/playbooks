Pre-Launch Checklist (Before Day 1)

Time Investment: 2-3 days
Budget: $200-500

Essential Setup

  • Business Foundation

    • Register business name/LLC (if not done)
    • Open business bank account
    • Set up accounting system (QuickBooks, Wave, or Xero)
    • Get business insurance (liability)

  • Financial Setup

    • Payment processor account (Stripe + PayPal)
    • Invoicing system ready
    • Refund policy drafted
    • Pricing structure finalized

  • Legal Compliance

    • Terms & Conditions template
    • Privacy Policy
    • Coaching agreement/contract template
    • GDPR/data compliance basics (if serving EU)

  • Core Tools Subscription

    • Domain name purchased (brand name)
    • Email hosting (Google Workspace or similar)
    • Password manager (1Password/LastPass)
    • Project management tool (Notion, ClickUp, or Asana)

  • Mindset & Preparation

    • Define your "why" and mission statement
    • Set revenue goals (90-day, 6-month, 1-year)
    • Block calendar time for this project
    • Inform family/support system of commitment

Budget Allocation: $200-500
Decision Point: If you can't commit 20+ hours/week for 90 days, defer launch until you can.

Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)

Objective: Build brand identity, create core offer, establish infrastructure
Time Investment: 25-35 hours/week
Budget: $2,500-4,000

Week 1: Brand, Positioning & Offer Structure (Days 1-7)

Weekly Budget: $500-800
Weekly Goal: Crystal-clear positioning and signature offer defined

Day 1 (Monday): Brand Foundation

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (2-3 hrs): Market research session

    • Analyze 10 competitors (pricing, offers, positioning)
    • Document what they do well and gaps you can fill
    • Define your unique mechanism/approach

  2. Afternoon (2-3 hrs): Avatar definition

    • Create detailed customer avatar (demographics, psychographics)
    • Identify their #1 pain point and desired outcome
    • List objections they'll have about your offer

  3. Evening (1 hr): Brand name brainstorm

    • Generate 20-30 brand name options
    • Check domain availability
    • Run by 3 trusted people for feedback

Deliverables:

  • Competitor analysis spreadsheet
  • Customer avatar document (1-2 pages)
  • Brand name shortlist (3-5 options)

Resources Needed:

  • Google Sheets/Docs
  • Namecheap or GoDaddy for domain checking
  • Access to competitor websites/social media

Quick Win: Choose and purchase your domain name today

Common Pitfall: Over-researching competitors and copying instead of differentiating

Day 2 (Tuesday): Positioning & Messaging

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (2-3 hrs): Positioning statement

    • Fill out positioning template: "I help [WHO] achieve [WHAT] through [UNIQUE METHOD] so they can [ULTIMATE BENEFIT]"
    • Write your origin story (why you're qualified)
    • Define your brand voice (professional/casual, warm/authoritative, etc.)

  2. Afternoon (2-3 hrs): Core messaging development

    • Write 3 headlines for different pain points
    • Create your elevator pitch (30 seconds, 2 minutes)
    • Draft your "About" page copy

  3. Evening (1-2 hrs): Visual identity kickoff

    • Choose 2-3 brand colors using Coolors.co
    • Select brand fonts (Google Fonts)
    • Create mood board (Canva or Pinterest)

Deliverables:

  • Positioning statement document
  • Elevator pitch script
  • Brand style guide (colors, fonts, tone)

Resources Needed:

  • Canva (free tier)
  • Google Fonts
  • Coolors.co

Quick Win: Post your elevator pitch to personal social media to test resonance

Common Pitfall: Being too generic ("I help people get jobs") instead of specific

Day 3 (Wednesday): Offer Structure Design

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (3 hrs): Signature offer creation

    • Define program length (30/60/90 days recommended)
    • Map out curriculum modules (4-8 modules)
    • Identify transformation milestones
    • Create program name

  2. Afternoon (2-3 hrs): Deliverables & format

    • Decide: group vs. 1-on-1 vs. hybrid
    • Plan session frequency (weekly? bi-weekly?)
    • List bonuses (templates, resources, community access)
    • Draft program overview (1-pager)

  3. Evening (1 hr): Pricing strategy

    • Research market rates ($1k-$5k+ range)
    • Calculate your minimum viable price
    • Create tiered options if applicable (good/better/best)
    • Decide on payment plans (full pay, 2-pay, 3-pay)

Deliverables:

  • Signature program curriculum outline
  • Program one-pager
  • Pricing structure document

Resources Needed:

  • Google Docs
  • Calculator for pricing math

Quick Win: Share your offer concept with 3 past colleagues for feedback

Common Pitfall: Underpricing due to impostor syndrome (don't charge less than $1,500 for transformation)

Day 4 (Thursday): Logo & Visual Identity

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (1 hr): Brief creation for designer

    • Write design brief with brand attributes
    • Gather 5-10 logo examples you like
    • Prepare brand colors/fonts from Day 2

  2. Afternoon (3-4 hrs): Designer outreach OR DIY

    • Option A: Hire designer on Fiverr ($50-150)
    • Option B: Create logo in Canva yourself (2-3 hours)
    • Request 3 concepts with variations

  3. Evening (1-2 hrs): Social media setup

    • Create business profiles: LinkedIn, YouTube, Instagram (optional)
    • Use temporary logo/banner
    • Write bio using positioning statement
    • Follow 50 relevant accounts in your niche

Deliverables:

  • Logo (primary + variations)
  • Social media profiles created
  • Brand assets folder organized

Resources Needed:

  • Fiverr or Canva Pro ($13/mo)
  • High-res headshot photo

Team Requirements:

  • Graphic designer (Fiverr): $50-150

Quick Win: Get your LinkedIn profile updated with new positioning today

Common Pitfall: Spending 2 weeks on logo perfection (80% solution is fine for now)

Deliverables:

  • Live website (basic version)

Resources Needed:

  • Systeme.io ($27/mo) or equivalent platform
  • Google Analytics account (free)

Budget Allocation: $27-100 (depending on platform)

Quick Win: Share your new website URL with 5 friends for feedback

Common Pitfall: Trying to build a perfect website before testing your offer

Resources Needed:

  3. Evening (1 hr): Delivery setup

    • Upload to Google Drive or Dropbox

Resources Needed:

Day 9 (Tuesday): Landing Page Build

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (3-4 hrs): Landing page copywriting

    • Write attention-grabbing headline
    • List 5-7 bullet points (benefits of lead magnet)
    • Create CTA button copy
    • Write social proof (if available) or authority statements
    • Formula: Headline + Subheadline + Benefits + CTA + Trust elements

  2. Afternoon (2-3 hrs): Landing page design

    • Create page in Systeme.io or Leadpages
    • Add opt-in form (name + email minimum)
    • Upload hero image or mockup
    • Mobile optimization check

  3. Evening (1 hr): A/B test setup

    • Create 2 headline variations
    • Set up split test (if platform allows)
    • Add Facebook Pixel and Google Analytics tracking

Deliverables:

  • Live landing page with opt-in form
  • A/B test variants ready
  • Short URL created (Bitly)

Resources Needed:

  • Systeme.io or Leadpages ($37-99/mo)
  • Stock photos (Unsplash/Pexels - free)

Budget Allocation: $37-99/mo for landing page platform

Quick Win: Get 5 people to opt-in today (friends/family test)

Common Pitfall: Overcomplicated landing pages with too much copy (keep it scannable)

Day 10 (Wednesday): Email Marketing Setup

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (2-3 hrs): Email platform setup

    • Choose platform: ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, or Systeme.io
    • Recommendation: ConvertKit (up to 1k subscribers free)

    • Create email signature with brand
    • Set up domain authentication (SPF, DKIM)

  2. Afternoon (3-4 hrs): Welcome sequence writing

    • Email 1: Deliver lead magnet + introduce yourself
    • Email 2 (Day 2): Share your story + establish authority
    • Email 3 (Day 4): Provide quick win tip
    • Email 4 (Day 6): Address common objection
    • Email 5 (Day 8): Soft pitch or survey

  3. Evening (1-2 hrs): Automation setup

    • Connect landing page to email platform
    • Set up automation trigger (new subscriber)
    • Schedule welcome sequence
    • Test end-to-end flow

Deliverables:

  • Email platform configured
  • 5-email welcome sequence written and automated
  • Test subscriber journey completed

Resources Needed:

  • ConvertKit (free up to 1k) or Systeme.io
  • Email copy templates

Quick Win: Send yourself through the funnel to experience it

  1. Morning (3 hrs): Webinar research

Day 23 (Tuesday): Webinar Script Writing (Part 1)

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (3-4 hrs): Hook & introduction section

    • Write opening hook (first 5 minutes - critical!)
    • Introduce yourself (2 minutes)
    • Set expectations and agenda
    • Get audience engagement (poll, chat)
    • Build authority and credibility

  2. Afternoon (3-4 hrs): Content section (Part 1)

    • Write teaching point #1 (12-15 min)
    • Include story/case study
    • Make it actionable
    • Build micro-commitments

  3. Evening (1 hr): Transitional content

    • Write transitions between sections
    • Add engagement checkpoints
    • Create chat prompts/polls

Deliverables:

  • Hook and intro script (5 min)
  • Teaching point #1 script (15 min)
  • Engagement elements

Resources Needed:

  • Webinar script template (Russell Brunson's Perfect Webinar or similar)
  • Timer for practice

Quick Win: Practice delivering the hook 10 times out loud

Common Pitfall: Writing a script that sounds robotic (write how you speak)

Day 24 (Wednesday): Webinar Script Writing (Part 2)

Daily Tasks:

  1. Morning (3-4 hrs): Content section (Parts 2 & 3)

    • Write teaching point #2 (12-15 min)
    • Write teaching point #3 (12-15 min)
    • Include stories and proof
    • Set up the pitch naturally

  2. Afternoon (3-4 hrs): Pitch section

    • Write pitch introduction
    • Present offer with urgency
    • Stack value (show all components)
    • Address objections (price, time, results, guarantee)
    • Create scarcity (bonuses, deadline, limited spots)

  3. Evening (1-2 hrs): Close and Q&A prep

    • Write strong close/CTA
    • Prepare FAQ answers (10-15 questions)
    • Create replay strategy

Deliverables:

  • Complete webinar script (60-90 min)
  • FAQ document
  • Pitch slide content

Resources Needed:

  • Objection research (survey, interviews)

Quick Win: Record yourself reading the script to check flow

Common Pitfall: Not practicing the pitch section (it's the most important part)
