6. SALES CALL SCRIPTS

Opening Script

YOU: "Hey [NAME], thanks for making the time today. How are you?"

THEM: [Response]

YOU: "Great. So I reviewed your application before this call, and I think there might be a really good fit here. But before we dive in, I want to make sure this is valuable for both of us. Can I ask you a few questions to understand your situation better?"

THEM: "Sure."

YOU: "Perfect. So on your application, you mentioned you're a [TITLE] earning around $[X]. Walk me through your current situation - what's working, what's not, and why you're considering remote work now?"

[Listen actively. Take notes. Let them talk for 5-7 minutes uninterrupted.]

YOU: "Got it. And you mentioned your timeline is [X] days. Tell me more about that - why that specific timeline? What happens if you DON'T make this transition by then?"

[Listen for urgency drivers: bad boss, company issues, family reasons, relocation, etc.]

YOU: "Makes sense. And just to be clear - you indicated you're comfortable investing $[X] in a solution. If we decide today that this is the right fit, is that budget available now, or would you need time to arrange it?"

[Listen for: "Ready now" = HOT. "Need to move some money" = WARM. "Need to talk to spouse" = PAUSE]

YOU: "Understood. Last question before I tell you about the program - on a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to making this transition? Like, if nothing changes, where will you be 6 months from now?"

[Listen for their number. If <7, probe: "What would it take to get you to a 10?"]

YOU: "Okay, great. So based on what you've shared, I think you'd be a great fit for the 6-Figure Remote Career Accelerator. Let me walk you through what that looks like..."

[Transition to pitch]

Objection Handler #1: "I can't afford it"

THEM: "I don't think I can afford $7,500 right now."

YOU: "I totally understand - that's a significant investment. Can I ask you a question?"

THEM: "Sure."

YOU: "If you land a $120K remote role - which is our average client outcome - that's $10,000 per month, right?"

THEM: "Yeah."

YOU: "So would the first month's salary cover this investment?"

THEM: "Well, yes, but‚Äî"

YOU: "And let's say you're currently at $75K. That's a $45K annual increase. Over 5 years, that's $225,000 more in earnings. Does that make sense?"

THEM: "Yes."

YOU: "So the real question isn't 'Can I afford $7,500?' It's 'Can I afford NOT to invest?' Because every month you stay in your current situation is costing you $3,750. How much have you already lost by waiting?"

[Pause. Let them think.]

YOU: "Plus, we have payment plans. You can do $2,500/month for 3 months. Would that work better?"

THEM: [Response]

[If still hesitant: "What's really holding you back? Is it the money, or something else?"]

Objection Handler #2: "I need to talk to my spouse"

THEM: "This sounds great, but I need to talk to my spouse first."

YOU: "Absolutely - I totally respect that. Can I ask, is your spouse supportive of you making this career transition?"

THEM: "Yes, very."

YOU: "Great. And what concerns do you think they might have that I can address right now?"

THEM: [Usually: cost, time commitment, legitimacy]

YOU: "Got it. So here's what I'd suggest: Let's get them on the phone right now. I'm happy to answer any questions they have directly. Does that work?"

THEM: "Uh, they're at work right now..."

YOU: "No problem. When can the three of us chat? I have openings at [TIME] or [TIME] today."

[If they resist:]

YOU: "Let me ask you this - if your spouse says yes, are YOU ready to move forward?"

THEM: "Well, yes."

YOU: "Okay, so the decision is really yours, and you just want their blessing. I get that. But here's what I've learned in coaching 500+ professionals: The people who 'need to think about it' or 'talk to someone else' are usually still in the same job 6 months later. The people who commit TODAY are the ones sending me offer screenshots in 60 days. Which group do you want to be in?"

[Pause.]

YOU: "Look, I'm not trying to pressure you. If you're not ready, that's okay. But I want you to be honest with yourself - is this really about your spouse, or are you using that as a reason to delay a decision you know you should make?"

[Direct, but said kindly. Often breaks through.]

Objection Handler #3: "What if it doesn't work for me?"

THEM: "This sounds good, but what if it doesn't work for me?"

YOU: "Great question. Let me be direct: The only way this doesn't work is if you don't do the work. Fair?"

THEM: "Yeah, I guess."

YOU: "We have a 90-day job offer guarantee. That means you either land a $100K+ offer in 90 days, or we keep working with you for FREE until you do. I take all the risk. The question is: Can you commit to showing up and following the system?"

THEM: "Yes."

YOU: "Good. Now, let me ask you something else - what makes you think it WON'T work?"

THEM: [Usually: age, industry, lack of remote experience, etc.]

YOU: "Got it. So you're worried about [specific concern]. Let me tell you about [CLIENT NAME]."

[Share a case study of someone with the same concern who succeeded.]

YOU: "They had the exact same doubt. And they crushed it. The system works if YOU work. So the real question is: Are you willing to trust the process?"

THEM: [Response]
