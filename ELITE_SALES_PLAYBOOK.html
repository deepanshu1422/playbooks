7.3 SPLIT TESTING

What to Test First (Highest Impact)

Priority 1: Webinar Offer

  • What you're selling (VIP vs Standard vs DIY)
  • Price point ($4,997 vs $3,997 vs $2,997)
  • Payment options (one-pay vs installments)
  • Bonuses (which ones, how many)

Impact: 30-50% lift in conversions

Priority 2: Webinar Close Sequence

  • When you transition from teaching to selling (minute 30 vs 40)
  • How many CTAs you give (5 vs 7 vs 10)
  • Urgency messaging (spot scarcity vs bonus expiration)
  • Application vs direct purchase

Impact: 20-30% lift

Priority 3: Post-Webinar Email Sequence

  • Subject lines
  • Send timing (immediate vs 2h later)
  • Number of emails (4 vs 6 vs 8)
  • Tone (urgent vs educational)

Impact: 10-20% lift

Priority 4: Sales Call Structure

  • Discovery depth (15 min vs 25 min)
  • Proof placement (when you show testimonials)
  • Closing technique (assumptive vs alternative)

Impact: 5-15% lift

Priority 5: Pre-Webinar Sequence

  • Reminder emails (2 vs 4 vs 6)
  • Commitment devices (homework vs survey)
  • Pre-webinar video (yes vs no)

Impact: 5-10% lift in show rate

Sample Sizes Needed

For Statistically Significant Results:

Test Minimum Sample Ideal Sample
Webinar offer/price 100 attendees per variant 200+ per variant
Email subject line 500 recipients per variant 1,000+ per variant
Sales call script 20 calls per variant 50+ per variant
Landing page 1,000 visitors per variant 5,000+ per variant

Rule of Thumb:

  • Need at least 30-50 conversions per variant for reliability
  • Run test for at least 2 weeks (accounts for day-of-week variance)
  • Don't stop test early even if one variant is winning

Testing Cadence

Weekly: Minor tweaks (email subject lines, CTA copy)
Bi-Weekly: Medium changes (email sequence structure)
Monthly: Major changes (webinar structure, offer changes)
Quarterly: Complete overhauls (new webinar, new sales process)

What NOT to Test:

  • Don't test multiple things at once (can't isolate what worked)
  • Don't test with small sample sizes (unreliable data)
  • Don't change tests mid-stream (invalidates results)

Testing Framework

Step 1: Hypothesis

  • "We believe that [CHANGE] will result in [OUTCOME] because [REASON]"
  • Example: "We believe that adding a 6-month payment plan will increase close rate by 15% because it lowers the barrier to entry"

Step 2: Design Test

  • Control (current method)
  • Variant (new method)
  • Traffic split (50/50)
  • Success metric (close rate)

Step 3: Run Test

  • Duration: 2-4 weeks
  • Monitor daily but don't make decisions until complete

Step 4: Analyze Results

  • Which variant won?
  • By how much? (% lift)
  • Is it statistically significant?

Step 5: Implement Winner

  • Roll out winning variant to 100%
  • Document learnings
  • Move to next test
