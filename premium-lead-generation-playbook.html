2. MULTI-STEP LANDING PAGES & QUALIFICATION FUNNELS

2.1 THE PSYCHOLOGY OF MULTI-STEP FUNNELS

Why Multi-Step Works for Premium Offers:

  • Commitment escalation: Each step increases psychological investment
  • Sunk cost fallacy: "I've already answered 5 questions, might as well finish"
  • Self-selection: Poor fits drop off early (saves you time)
  • Data collection: More nuanced lead scoring than single opt-in
  • Positioning: Application = exclusive, single opt-in = mass market

The Kennedy "Reason Why" Principle:
At each step, explain WHY you're asking (builds trust, reduces resistance)

8.4 WHEN TO PUSH HARD VS BACK OFF

Push Hard When:

  • They've engaged multiple times (opened 5+ emails, watched webinar, visited pricing)
  • They're in your Platinum or Gold segment
  • They've indicated urgency (<60 day timeline)
  • They've asked questions (engagement = interest)
  • Time-sensitive offer is expiring (cohort starting, price increase, limited spots)

How to Push:

  • Daily emails (not annoying if valuable)
  • Multiple CTAs per email
  • Urgency language ("Last chance," "Spots filling," "Price increases Monday")
  • Direct questions ("What's holding you back?")
  • Phone/SMS follow-up (if they opted in)

Back Off When:

  • They've unsubscribed or marked as spam
  • They explicitly said "not interested"
  • They're in Bronze segment (dormant)
  • Open rate <5% over 10 emails (they're not reading)
  • They requested fewer emails

How to Back Off:

  • Move to monthly broadcast-only
  • Send re-engagement campaign ("Should I keep emailing?")
  • Offer to unsubscribe or change frequency
  • Stop direct outreach (email only, no calls/texts)

CONCLUSION: THE PREMIUM POSITIONING MINDSET

Remember:

  1. Quality > Quantity: 10 perfect-fit clients at $7,500 each ($75K) beats 100 mediocre leads at $500 each ($50K) - and requires way less headache.

  2. Repel to Attract: The more you repel wrong-fit leads (broke, uncommitted, tire-kickers), the more you attract right-fit leads (wealthy, decisive, action-takers).

  3. Price = Filter: Your price should make some people uncomfortable. If everyone says "yes" easily, you're too cheap.

  4. Authority > Persuasion: Build so much authority that clients chase YOU, not the other way around. (Media features, case studies, social proof)

  5. Systems > Hustle: Don't grind harder, build better systems. (Lead scoring, automation, disqualification criteria)

  6. Specificity Sells: "I help working professionals earning $80K+ land $150K remote roles in 90 days" beats "I help people find remote jobs."

  7. Guarantee > Hype: Don't just promise results, GUARANTEE them. (Hormozi: "Remove risk to increase conversions")

  8. Nurture = Sieve: Your email sequences should filter, not convince. The right people will self-select in.

  9. Say No More: The more you're willing to say "You're not a fit," the more respect you earn from ideal clients.

  10. Invest in Yourself: If you're selling a $7,500 program, you should be investing $10K+ in YOUR education, coaching, tools. Walk the walk.
