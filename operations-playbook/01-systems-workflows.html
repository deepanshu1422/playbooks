Video Recording Workflow

DAILY BATCH RECORDING (2-3 hours)

Best for: Short-form content, tips, Q&A

Preparation (15 minutes):

  1. Review content calendar for today's topics
  2. Gather 5-7 topic ideas (from customer questions, trends, keyword research)
  3. Create simple bullet-point outlines (not scripts)
  4. Set up recording space:
    • Clean background
    • Ring light or natural light
    • Lapel mic or quality microphone
    • Camera at eye level
    • Phone on silent

Recording (90-120 minutes):

  1. Record 5-7 videos back-to-back (10-15 min each)
  2. One take per video (authentic > perfect)
  3. Save each video with naming convention: YYYY-MM-DD_Topic-Keyword_V1.mp4
  4. Quick review: audio clear? video quality good? If yes, move on.

Post-Recording (30 minutes):

  1. Upload raw files to Google Drive folder: Content/Raw-Footage/YYYY-MM/
  2. Fill out Video Brief Template for editor (see templates/)
  3. Update content calendar with "In Editing" status
  4. Slack/email editor with batch details

Output: 5-7 videos ready for editing (weekly = 25-35 videos/month)

WEEKLY BATCH RECORDING (4-5 hours)

Best for: Long-form content, tutorials, case studies

Preparation (30 minutes):

  1. Choose 2-3 in-depth topics for the week
  2. Create detailed outlines with examples, case studies, actionable steps
  3. Prep slides if needed (Canva template)
  4. Gather props (resumes to review, job postings, etc.)

Recording (3 hours):

  1. Record 2-3 long-form videos (20-40 min each)
  2. Allow for retakes on complex sections
  3. Record multiple intro/outro variations for testing
  4. Capture B-roll footage (typing, working, etc.)

Post-Recording (1 hour):

  1. Upload to Content/Raw-Footage-Long/YYYY-MM/
  2. Detailed video brief for editor
  3. Mark high-value clips for repurposing
  4. Update content calendar

Output: 2-3 pillar videos/week (8-12/month)

MONTHLY BATCH RECORDING (Full day)

Best for: Course content, paid program materials, evergreen series

Preparation (1-2 days before):

  1. Finalize course outline/module structure
  2. Script key sections (intros, CTAs, complex explanations)
  3. Create all slides/visuals
  4. Test all tech (camera, mics, lighting, screen recording)
  5. Block full day on calendar (no interruptions)

Recording Day (6-8 hours):

  1. Record full course/module (typically 2-4 hours of finished content)
  2. Take breaks every 45-60 minutes
  3. Re-record sections as needed
  4. Capture multiple CTA variations

Post-Recording (2 hours):

  1. Organize all files by module/section
  2. Detailed editing brief with timestamps
  3. Upload to dedicated folder
  4. Schedule editing timeline with editor

Output: Complete course/program content (10-20 hours of raw footage ‚Üí 4-8 hours edited)

Purpose: Maximize attendance

  • Email 1 (Registration confirmed): Thank you + calendar add
  • Email 2 (Day before): Reminder + what to prep
  • Email 3 (2 hours before): "Starting soon" + Zoom link
  • Email 4 (10 min before): "We're live!" + urgency
  • Email 5 (Replay - if no-show): Missed it? Here's the replay (24-48 hour deadline)

Purpose: Convert webinar attendees

  • Email 1 (Same day): Thank you + slides/resources
  • Email 2 (Day 1): Address objection #1 (price)

  • Email 4 (Day 3): Urgency (bonuses expiring)

Deliverability Monitoring

DELIVERABILITY CHECKLIST

Domain Setup:

  • SPF record configured
  • DKIM record configured
  • DMARC record configured
  • Custom sending domain (mail.yourdomain.com)
  • Branded "From" address (you@yourdomain.com, not @gmail.com)

List Quality:

  • Double opt-in enabled (best practice)
  • Unsubscribe link clear and functional
  • Physical mailing address in footer (CAN-SPAM requirement)
  • No purchased/scraped lists (ever)

Content Quality:

  • Spam score <5 (use Mail-Tester.com)
  • Balanced text-to-image ratio (more text than images)
  • No excessive CAPS or exclamation marks!!!
  • No spam trigger words ("free money," "act now," etc.)
  • Personalization tokens working (no [FNAME] errors)

Engagement:

  • Open rate >20% (aim for 25-35%)
  • Click rate >2% (aim for 3-5%)
  • Bounce rate <2%
  • Spam complaint rate <0.1%
  • Unsubscribe rate <0.5%

Monitoring Tools:

  • Google Postmaster Tools - Gmail deliverability
  • Mail-Tester.com - Spam score testing
  • GlockApps or Litmus - Inbox placement testing

Red Flags:

  • Sudden drop in open rates (>10% decrease)
  • High bounce rate (>5%)
  • Emails landing in spam (test with seed list)
  • Blacklist warnings (check MXToolbox.com)

Fixes:

  • Warm up new sending domain (start small, increase gradually)
  • Remove hard bounces immediately
  • Improve subject lines (less "salesy")
  • Increase engagement (send to engaged segment first)
  • Contact ESP support if issues persist

A/B Testing Protocols

WHAT TO TEST

Priority Order:

  1. Subject Lines (biggest impact on opens)
  2. CTA Copy/Placement (biggest impact on clicks)
  3. Email Length (short vs. long)
  4. Send Time (morning vs. afternoon vs. evening)
  5. Personalization (with name vs. without)
  6. Content Format (story vs. tip vs. case study)
  7. Sender Name (founder name vs. brand name vs. "Founder at Brand")

A/B TEST SETUP

Subject Line Testing (Every Email):

  1. Write 2-3 subject line variations
  2. Set up A/B split in ESP:
    • 50/50 split (Variation A vs. B)
    • Send to random sample
    • Winning metric: Open rate
  3. Run test for 2-4 hours or until statistical significance
  4. Winner automatically sends to remaining list

CTA Testing (Monthly):

  1. Choose one email type (e.g., webinar invite)
  2. Create 2 versions with different CTAs:
    • Variation A: "Save My Seat ‚Üí"
    • Variation B: "Yes, I Want to Attend"
  3. 50/50 split
  4. Track clicks to landing page
  5. Implement winner going forward

Send Time Testing (Quarterly):

  1. Choose standard broadcast email
  2. Send identical email to 4 segments at different times:
    • 8:00 AM
    • 12:00 PM
    • 5:00 PM
    • 8:00 PM
  3. Track opens and clicks for 48 hours
  4. Identify optimal send time for your audience
  5. Update default send time

TESTING BEST PRACTICES

  • One variable at a time (don't test subject + CTA simultaneously)
  • Statistical significance (wait for at least 100 opens per variation)
  • Document results (maintain A/B test log in spreadsheet)
  • Implement learnings (update templates with winners)
  • Retest quarterly (audience preferences change)
